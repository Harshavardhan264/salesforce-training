# 🚀 Salesforce CRM Basics

## 1️⃣ What is CRM?

CRM stands for **Customer Relationship Management**.

> CRM is a system or software used by companies to manage customers, store customer details, track conversations, sales, and improve customer service.

### 📌 Example

Imagine a shop owner has many customers.  
Instead of writing customer information in notebooks, they use a CRM tool to store:

- Customer names
- Phone numbers
- Emails
- Products purchased
- Complaints or requests
- Follow-up reminders

This helps the business maintain good relationships with customers.

---

## ✅ Why CRM is Used

- Organize customer data
- Improve customer support
- Increase sales
- Track leads and deals
- Communicate with customers easily

---

# 2️⃣ Why Companies Use Salesforce?

Companies use Salesforce to:

- Store customer data
- Track sales
- Improve customer service
- Automate business tasks

> Salesforce helps companies manage customer relationships, improve sales, provide better customer support, and automate business processes efficiently.

---

## ✅ Main Reasons

### 📌 Centralized Customer Data

All customer information is stored in one place.

### 📌 Efficiency & Automation

Automates repetitive tasks like emails, reminders, and approvals.

### 📌 Scalability & Flexibility

Salesforce can grow with the company’s needs.

### 📌 Innovation & ROI

Helps businesses make better decisions and increase profits.

---

# 3️⃣ Explain Salesforce Objects

## 🏢 a) Account

An **Account** represents a company, organization, or customer in Salesforce.

### 📌 Examples

- TCS
- Infosys
- Amazon

### 📌 Account Stores

- Company name
- Address
- Phone number
- Industry details

---

## 👤 b) Contact

A **Contact** represents a person associated with an Account.

### 📌 Example

If the Account is Amazon, the Contacts can be:

- Ravi Kumar – Manager
- Priya Sharma – HR

### 📌 Contact Stores

- Name
- Email
- Phone number
- Job role

---

## 💼 c) Opportunity

An **Opportunity** represents a potential sales deal or business opportunity.

It helps track:

- Sales progress
- Revenue
- Deal status

### 📌 Example

A company wants to buy software worth ₹5 lakhs.  
This sales deal is stored as an Opportunity.

### 📌 Opportunity Includes

- Deal amount
- Stage of sale
- Closing date
- Probability of winning

---

# 4️⃣ Real-World Mapping

| Salesforce Object | Real-World Meaning | Example |
|-------------------|-------------------|---------|
| 🏢 Account | Company/Organization | Infosys |
| 👤 Contact | Person working in the company | Ravi Kumar – Manager |
| 💼 Opportunity | Business deal or sales chance | Software deal worth ₹10 lakhs |

---

## 📌 Simple Real-Life Flow

```text
Company → Person → Deal
Account → Contact → Opportunity
```
